Vietnam's healthcare system has undergone significant transformations and faced various challenges as it tries to achieve the global health insurance range (UHC). As of 2020, about 90.85% of Vietnam's population is paid by social health insurance, a notable achievement emphasizing the government's commitment to making universal health affordable. Public hospitals, accounting for 86% of all hospitals, are particularly affected, with facilities in major cities operating at twice as much capacity. The Ministry of Health has also updated regulations to improve healthcare supply and access. Vietnam's healthcare system is at an important time, with significant achievements in expanding health insurance and regulatory reform. However, challenges such as overcrowding in hospitals, outdated equipment and financial sustainability remain. Further, the government is exploring ways to provide a package of evidence-based benefits through social health insurance and develop a more effective healthcare distribution system.
